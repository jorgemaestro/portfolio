User Journey

Brief

Fakulty is born from the result of a heavy research. The problem statement had to be created from the very beggining. Nor before being validated by Ironhack’s Direction.

Out of the 6 problems that I put on the table, we all agreed that digging deeper into the idea of connecting and empowering a certain community seemed to be the right direction.

The challenge

At this point, the idea seemed right, but there were still too many points to be precised. Developing an idea that worked for everybody, was still something hard to tackle; It needed definition.

My priority was to come up with a sharper problem statement. Something certain where I could put the focus on. I must affect a precise group of people or community.

Research

Students, universities... Overall, education seemed to be the perfect deal. Students are the present, the past and the future of any society.

Both surveys and interviews were essential when it came to start developing this product. They prooffed that there's an actual problem among the students community. They feel frustrated and uncapable of facing certain situations because there's a lack of coordination or dependency of certain people or institutions.

Most of students think delegations barely work. There's a real frustration when it comes to propose anything to their universities.

Create

Prototype

The following video shows the full process since the user logs-in and create an event related to all universities within the entire region of Madrid.

At the beggning the user is capable of filter its content so he can actually visualize the most relevant information. Placed at the top, there's a personalized calendar where the user will decide what events, publications or meetings have been scheduled ahead.

The rest is about controlling the impact of the event and setting features such as place, time, picture, title, description... Once the user double-checks (in the preview section) that eveything is correct, the new proposal is ready to be posted. All students that match with its labels will receive instantly a notification with it.

Since the time given for this projects was just 2 weeks, I only had the chance to focus on developing what I considered one of the most important features. However, there are tools such as surveys and proposals that are not displayed yet that offer an infinte number of possibilities and interactions among the student's community.

Insights

Stoppers

With no doubt one of the biggest challenges was to be precise with the problem I wanted to tackle. It’s been said that sometimes the “How” is not actually the problem but the “What” and that’s what stopped me the most at the bigging of this challenge.

I didn’t only have to be clear with the idea but with the design and the functions that this first MVP would include.

It took me a while and work to figure out the direction of UI Design. I understood this project as something disruptive and so, It had to look like. Even though it took me a while to define it, I am happy with the “brutalist” style that I’ve come up with. Expressing the kind of emotions that I looked for.

Conclusions

Fakulty gave me the opportunity to apply a big part of the knowledge that I acquired during the last few months. Even though there’s always room for improvement, I can proudly admit that I ended up pretty satisfied with both the result and my way along the entire process.

The major lessons that I took out of this project:

  1. There are lots of ideas and projects that have already solved many of my current problems. Looking out there and learning from successful cases is a must, almost in any aspect of your life.
  2. Share, share, share… and talk. Sometimes, we are so long working in our own project that we become unable to see things that others may do in a second.
  3. Finally, the importance of making decisions at the right time. Knowing when it’s the right moment to move forward, even though you feel it’s not perfect. it’s essential to progress and getting things done. There will be always time to think, analyze and keep improving.
