We identified in our surveys and interviews that most of people were missing the idea of being able to share their “reading” with someone the same way they do it when they consume platforms like Netflix.

A lot of readers feel like sharing their thoughts or their feelings after having read or listened to a story. It is precisely in the social act of sharing where we realized we had to put the focus on.

User persona

Most of students think delegations barely work. There's a real frustration when it comes to propose anything to their universities.

We all love stories but the way we consume them is absolutely different. Our user persona is used to read books on a daily basis. They tend to have an active social life and is not over his or her 40’s.

“It feels better when I listen to my favourite book with someone. It’s like watching Netflix”

“How might we transform the experience of listening to stories by one-self into something more social?”

Insights

Stoppers

There were to major stoppers during the duration of this project.

The first one, and most obvious, it was to be able to identify what element might be missing this product. In order to figure this out, research and interviews turned to be key tools when it comes to create and produce.

At the beginning we thought that brainstorming was going to help us a lot while we learned that it was only when we started to talk and ask real users of this kind of products when we started to identify areas of improvement.

The second one was to agree among the team members in which direction we should start working. After a couple of hours of discussion, we managed to control the situation and decided among all of our ideas, which one was the most suitable.

Conclusions

While we didn’t have to put so much focus on the UI, since it was about an existing project with its existing colors and components… we did have to identify what feature would improve the current user experience.

Adding a new feature to an existing product is a lot about research and testing. It is about getting to know the users, their pains and their wishes.

Usually, when you have been able to identify the “what”, the “how” usually comes along with it.
